Unit 1 Assessment — Science & Observing the Natural World
🔭 Module 1 · Observation Notebook

This assessment shows what you learned about how scientists observe the world. You will tell the difference between observations and inferences, explain how scientists collect evidence, and show your own science thinking.

12 points

Observations & Inferences
4 points — Choose the best answer.
Observe or Infer? 1 pt
1A scientist at Yellowstone National Park writes: "The water in the hot spring is a bright blue-green color." What type of statement is this?
  • AAn inference — it is an explanation based on evidence
  • BAn observation — it is something the scientist noticed with her senses
  • CA prediction — it is a guess about what will happen
  • DA question — it is something the scientist wonders about
Observations → Inferences 1 pt
2A park scientist sees claw marks high on a tree trunk and fur caught on the bark. She writes: "A large bear scratched this tree." What is her statement?
  • AAn observation, because she can see the claw marks
  • BAn inference, because she did not see the bear — she used clues to figure it out
  • CA prediction, because she is guessing about the future
  • DData, because it is a number from a measurement
Science Tools 1 pt
3A scientist wants to record exactly how tall a seedling grew over one week. Which tool would give her the most accurate measurement?
  • AHer eyes alone — she will guess by looking at it
  • BA ruler — she will measure the height in centimeters
  • CA thermometer — she will check the temperature
  • DA magnet — she will test if the plant is attracted to it
Science Notebooks 1 pt
4Why do scientists record their observations in a notebook using drawings, labels, and words?
  • ASo they can share pretty pictures with their friends
  • BSo they have an accurate record of evidence they can look back at and use to explain their thinking
  • CBecause their teacher told them to and there is no other reason
  • DSo they can memorize every detail without thinking about it

Observe & Record
4 points — Use your senses and science skills to answer.
Use Your Senses 1 pt
5A scientist picks up a rock and describes it. Write down THREE observations about any object near you right now — one for each of three different senses.
💡 Start with: "I see ___, I feel ___, I hear ___." (or smell/taste where safe!)
Observation → Inference 1 pt
6You walk into a kitchen and notice: the oven is warm, there is a smell of bread, and the counter has flour on it. Write ONE observation and ONE inference from these clues.
💡 Start with: "My observation is ___. My inference is ___ because ___."
Scientific Sketch 1 pt
7A good scientific sketch is different from an art drawing. Describe what makes a scientific sketch useful to a scientist. What does it need to include?
💡 Start with: "A scientific sketch needs ___ because ___."
Collect Data 1 pt
8A park scientist observes a bird nest every day for five days. She records the number of eggs in the nest each day: Day 1 = 2 eggs, Day 2 = 3 eggs, Day 3 = 3 eggs, Day 4 = 4 eggs, Day 5 = 4 eggs. What pattern do you notice in her data? What might she infer from this pattern?
💡 Start with: "I notice that ___. This might mean ___."
