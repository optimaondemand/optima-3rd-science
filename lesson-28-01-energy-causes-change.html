📖 The Lesson: Energy Causes Change
Read and think carefully — science is all about noticing!
⏱ 12 min
🌱
Section 1
What Is Energy?

EnergyEnergyThe ability to do work or cause change. is what makes things happen. Without energy, nothing would move, light up, or make sound.

You cannot always see energy, but you can see what it does. When a ball rolls down a hill, energy is making it move. When the Sun warms your face, energy is at work.

🤔
Stop and Think
"Can you think of something that uses energy right now? How do you know energy is involved?"
🌤
Section 2
Forms of Energy

Energy comes in many forms. Light energyLight EnergyEnergy you can see. lets us see. Heat energyHeat EnergyEnergy that makes things warmer. makes things warmer. Sound energySound EnergyEnergy you can hear. is what we hear.

Motion energyMotion EnergyEnergy of movement. is the energy of things that are moving. Electrical energyElectrical EnergyEnergy that flows through wires. powers our lights, computers, and fans.

🌍
Section 3
Energy Causes Change

Energy always causes change. When you push a swing, your energy makes it move. When you turn on a lamp, electrical energy changes into light energy. When you clap your hands, motion energy becomes sound energy.

Real-World Connection: The Sun gives off light and heat energy. That heat energy warms Earth, melts ice, and evaporates water. Energy from the Sun drives weather, keeps plants growing, and keeps us warm.

⭐ Key Concept

Energy comes in many forms — light, heat, sound, motion, and electrical. Energy always causes change: it makes things move, warm up, glow, or make sound.

🤯
Amazing Nature Fact
A single bolt of lightning contains enough energy to toast 100,000 slices of bread — all in a fraction of a second!
Curious? Energy Changes Form

Energy can change from one form to another. When you turn on a flashlight, electrical energy becomes light energy. When you rub your hands together, motion energy becomes heat energy.

A guitar turns motion energy (strumming) into sound energy. A toaster turns electrical energy into heat energy.

Energy does not disappear — it just changes form. This is one of the most important ideas in all of science!

Now test the idea yourself.

⚡ Energy Scavenger Hunt
Find and classify forms of energy all around you
⏱ 15–20 min
🎒

You'll need: Science notebook, pencil, colored pencils

Follow each step carefully. Record your observations in your science notebook as you go.

1

👀 Step 1: Look Around: Look around the room (or think about your home). Find at least six things that use energy. Write the name of each item in your notebook.

2

📋 Step 2: Classify: Next to each item, write what form of energy it uses: light, heat, sound, motion, or electrical. Some items may use more than one form!

3

🔄 Step 3: Energy Changes: Pick three of your items. For each one, describe how energy changes form. Example: A lamp changes electrical energy into light energy.

4

📊 Step 4: Make a Chart: Create a chart sorting your items by energy type. Which form of energy did you find the most? Write one sentence about the pattern you noticed.

🌲
Think Like a Scientist
"Which form of energy did you find most often? Why do you think that form of energy is so common?"

🧠 Quick Self-Check
Not graded — just for you! See how much you remember before starting the notebook work.
1. Which of these is a form of energy?
AWeight
BHeat
CColor
🌲 Correct! Heat is a form of energy. Other forms include light, sound, motion, and electrical energy.
Think about what makes things move, warm up, or glow. Which option describes something that causes change?
2. What energy change happens when you turn on a flashlight?
AElectrical energy changes to light energy
BSound energy changes to heat energy
CLight energy changes to motion energy
🌲 Yes! A flashlight uses electrical energy from batteries and changes it into light energy.
A flashlight uses batteries. What form of energy do batteries have? What does the flashlight produce?
3. A student rubs her hands together quickly and they feel warm. What happened?
ASound energy was created
BLight energy changed to heat energy
CMotion energy changed to heat energy
🌲 Exactly! Rubbing your hands is motion, and that motion energy changes into heat energy through friction.
Rubbing is a type of movement. What form of energy is movement? What form of energy is warmth?
