Adaptations 1 pt
2A roseate spoonbill has a wide, flat bill that it swings back and forth through shallow water to scoop up small fish and shrimp. This is an example of —
  • AAn inherited disease
  • BA physical adaptation that helps the bird find food in its habitat
  • CA behavior learned from watching other animals
  • DA change caused by water pollution
Food Web 1 pt
3In the Everglades, sawgrass is eaten by apple snails, which are eaten by snail kites. The snail kite can ONLY eat apple snails. What would most likely happen if apple snails disappeared from the Everglades?
  • ASnail kites would easily find a different food source
  • BThe sawgrass population would immediately shrink
  • CSnail kite populations would struggle and likely decline without their food source
  • DNothing would change in the Everglades ecosystem
Ecosystem Threats 1 pt
4Burmese pythons are an invasive species in the Everglades. They eat native animals like rabbits, birds, and deer. Which statement BEST explains why this is a threat to the ecosystem?
  • APythons are the same size as native animals, so there is no change
  • BPythons are helping native animals by eating extra plants
  • CPythons compete for resources and reduce native animal populations, disrupting the food web
  • DPythons only eat plants, so animals are not affected
