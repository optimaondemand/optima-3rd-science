Forces & Motion
4 points — Choose the best answer.
Push or Pull? 1 pt
1Two students push a box from opposite sides with the same strength. The box does not move. What can you say about the forces?
  • AThe forces are unbalanced — the box should move
  • BThere are no forces acting on the box
  • CThe forces are balanced — equal forces from opposite directions cancel out
  • DThe box is too heavy for forces to affect it
Changing Motion 1 pt
2A soccer player kicks a ball hard. The ball rolls fast, then slows down and stops. What force slows the ball after the kick?
  • AThe kick — it keeps pushing the ball forward
  • BFriction from the ground — it pushes back against the ball's motion
  • CGravity — it pulls the ball upward
  • DThe ball's color makes it slow down
Magnets 1 pt
3You hold the north pole of one magnet near the north pole of another magnet. What happens?
  • AThe magnets attract — they pull toward each other
  • BThe magnets repel — they push away from each other
  • CNothing happens — magnets only react to metal objects
  • DThe magnets melt together
Magnetic Materials 1 pt
4A student tests four objects with a magnet: a steel paper clip, a plastic button, an iron nail, and a wooden stick. Which objects will the magnet attract?
  • AAll four objects — magnets attract everything
  • BOnly the plastic button and wooden stick
  • COnly the steel paper clip and iron nail
  • DNone of the objects — magnets only attract other magnets

Motion Data & Patterns
4 points — Use data to explain motion.
Ramp Data 1 pt
5A student rolls a ball down ramps at three heights and measures how far the ball travels: Low ramp = 20 cm, Medium ramp = 35 cm, High ramp = 52 cm. What pattern do you notice? What does this tell you about force and motion?
💡 Start with: "I notice that as the ramp height increases, the ball ___. This tells me that ___."
Repeating Motion 1 pt
6A pendulum swings back and forth. A student times it for 5 swings: 2 sec, 2 sec, 2 sec, 2 sec, 2 sec. Why is it useful that the motion repeats in the same pattern every time?
💡 Start with: "Repeating motion is useful because ___. Scientists can use this pattern to ___."
Magnetic Force 1 pt
7A magnet picks up a paper clip without touching it. What does this show about how magnetic force works? How is this different from a push or pull you do with your hand?
💡 Start with: "This shows that magnetic force can ___. This is different from a hand push because ___."
Engineering Challenge 1 pt
8A student builds a magnetic rescue tool to pick up metal objects from a container. Her first design only picks up 2 objects at a time. She wraps extra wire around the magnet — now it picks up 5. What did she do, and why did it work?
💡 Start with: "She changed her design by ___. It worked because ___."
