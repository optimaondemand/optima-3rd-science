📖 The Lesson: Engineering and Florida's Wild Places
Read through this before you start your design challenge.
⏱ 10 min
🌿
Section 1
From Problem to Solution

In the last lesson, you identified three big threats to Florida's ecosystems: pollutionPollutionHarmful materials that enter the air, water, or soil — often from runoff or waste., invasive species, and habitat lossHabitat LossWhen the natural home of a plant or animal is destroyed or changed so much animals can no longer live there.. People caused those problems — and people are now designing solutions.

That's what engineers do. They look at a real problem, figure out exactly what needs to be fixed, and design something to help.

🤔
Stop and Think
"Which of the three threats do you think is hardest to fix with a design? Why?"
⚙️
Section 2
The Engineering Design Process

Before any engineer builds anything, they follow a process. Every step matters. Skipping a step usually means the design fails.

The five steps are: Define the problem → Research what you need to know → Design a solution → Test it → Improve it. You'll use all five steps in your challenge today.

🏗️
Section 3
Real Engineers, Real Florida

Engineers in Florida have already built some creative solutions. They've built underwater habitat corridorHabitat CorridorA path that connects two wild areas so animals can travel safely between them.s, planted native trees along highway edges, and designed restorationRestorationBringing a damaged ecosystem back to a healthier state — usually by removing threats and replanting natives. marshes that clean polluted water naturally.

Real-World Connection: Engineers built 24 wildlife underpasses beneath Florida highways. After the underpasses opened, Florida panther road deaths dropped significantly.

🗺 Tap any part of this real Florida underpass to learn why engineers designed it that way
Tap any numbered zone to learn why engineers made that design choice.
A cross-section of a real Florida wildlife underpass — five design decisions, each solving a specific problem. What do you notice about how the criteria shaped each choice?
⭐ Key Concept

Good engineering design starts with a clear problem and works within real limits. Criteria tell you what success looks like. Constraints tell you what you have to work within.

Testing and improving are just as important as the first sketch.

🤯
Amazing Engineering Fact
Florida has built more than 24 wildlife underpasses and crossings beneath its highways. The Florida panther population — once below 30 individuals — has grown to over 200 today. Engineering helped save a species.
Curious? Go Deeper

The wildlife underpasses built in Florida's Picayune Strand area were engineered specifically for large cats. Engineers studied bobcat and panther movement patterns for over two years before finalizing the designs.

The crossings are wide enough for the animal to see through to the other side before entering — a key design criterion, because large cats won't enter a dark enclosed space. Vegetation planted at both ends reduces the contrast between open land and the underpass entrance.

One location, Alligator Alley, now has 48 underpasses. Camera monitoring shows deer, black bears, Florida panthers, bobcats, and alligators all using them regularly.

🧠 Quick Self-Check
Not graded — just for you! Check what stuck.
1. What does "criteria" mean in engineering design?
AThe limits on your design, like cost or materials
BThe features a solution must have to work well
CA test you run on a prototype to find problems
✅ Exactly! Criteria tell you what success looks like — what the solution must do.
Criteria and constraints are easy to mix up. Criteria = what success looks like. Try again!
2. An engineer designing a water filter must use only natural materials and spend under $20. What does "natural materials and under $20" describe?
AThe criteria — what the design must do
BThe prototype — a test version of the filter
CThe constraints — the limits the engineer must work within
🌿 Right! Limits on materials and cost are constraints — the engineer has to work within them.
Think about the word "limits." Constraints are limits. Which answer describes a limit? Try again!
3. An engineer tests her wildlife underpass and discovers animals won't use it. What should she do next?
AGive up — if the first design failed, the problem is too hard
BStart over from scratch with a completely new idea
CImprove the design based on what the test showed
⭐ Yes! Testing shows what needs to change. Then you improve — that's Step 5 of the process.
Think about the engineering design process. What comes after testing? Review the diagram and try again.
