📖 The Lesson: Light and Heat Investigation
Read and think carefully — science is all about noticing!
⏱ 12 min
🌱
Section 1
How Light Travels

LightLight Travels StraightLight moves in straight lines called rays. always travels in straight lines. That is why you can see a beam of light from a flashlight — it goes in one direction until it hits something.

When light hits a surface, two things can happen: it bounces off or it gets soaked up. What happens depends on the surface.

🤔
Stop and Think
"Why do you think flashlight beams look like straight lines? What happens when the beam hits a wall?"
🌤
Section 2
Reflection and Absorption

ReflectionReflectionWhen light bounces off a surface. happens when light bounces off a surface. Mirrors, calm water, and shiny metal are great reflectors. The light hits and bounces back toward your eyes.

AbsorptionAbsorptionWhen a material takes in light energy. happens when a material takes in the light energy instead of reflecting it. Dark colors absorb more light and turn it into heat. That is why dark surfaces feel warmer in the sun.

🌍
Section 3
Friction and Heat

Heat does not only come from the Sun. FrictionFrictionThe force created when two surfaces rub together. also creates heat energy. When you rub your hands together, the friction between your skin makes heat. The faster you rub, the warmer your hands get.

Real-World Connection: Tires on a road create friction, and that friction creates heat. After a long drive, car tires are warm to the touch because of all that friction.

⭐ Key Concept

Light travels in straight lines. It can be reflected (bounced off) or absorbed (taken in). Dark surfaces absorb more light and get warmer. Friction between surfaces also creates heat.

🤯
Amazing Nature Fact
A mirror reflects about 95% of the light that hits it — but a perfectly black surface absorbs almost 100%, turning all that light into heat!
Curious? Why Dark Roads Get Hot

On a summer day, have you ever noticed how hot a dark parking lot is compared to a light-colored sidewalk? The dark asphalt absorbs most of the sunlight that hits it. All that absorbed light turns into heat energyHeat TransferHeat moves from warmer to cooler objects..

The light-colored concrete reflects more light back into the air, so it stays cooler. This is why many cities are painting rooftops white — to reflect sunlight and keep buildings cooler.

Color matters! The color of a surface tells you a lot about whether it reflects or absorbs light.

💡 Light and Heat Exploration
Investigate how light interacts with different surfaces
⏱ 15–20 min
🎒

You'll need: Science notebook, pencil, colored pencils

Follow each step carefully. Record your observations in your science notebook as you go.

1

🔦 Step 1: Light Travels Straight: Imagine you shine a flashlight at a wall. Draw the beam in your notebook — it goes in a straight line. Now draw what happens when you hold a book in front of the beam. The light stops. Label your drawing.

2

🪞 Step 2: Reflection Test: Imagine you shine a flashlight at a mirror, a white piece of paper, and a black piece of paper. Draw what happens to the light for each surface. Which one reflects the most? Which reflects the least?

3

🌡️ Step 3: Absorption and Heat: Imagine two cups of water in the sun — one wrapped in black paper, one in white paper. Predict which cup will get warmer. Draw and label your prediction. The dark cup absorbs more light and heats up faster.

4

🤲 Step 4: Friction Test: Rub your hands together slowly for 5 seconds, then fast for 5 seconds. Which felt warmer? Draw and describe what happened. Friction turns motion energy into heat energy.

🌲
Think Like a Scientist
"Which surfaces reflected the most light? Which absorbed the most? What pattern do you see between color and heat?"

🧠 Quick Self-Check
Not graded — just for you! See how much you remember before starting the notebook work.
1. What happens when light hits a mirror?
AThe light is reflected — it bounces off the surface
BThe light is absorbed — it is taken in by the mirror
CThe light passes through the mirror
🌲 Correct! Mirrors are smooth and shiny, so they reflect light — the light bounces off the surface.
Think about what you see in a mirror. If the light were absorbed, could you see your reflection?
2. Why does a dark-colored surface feel warmer in the sun than a light-colored surface?
ADark surfaces reflect more light energy
BDark surfaces absorb more light energy and convert it to heat
CDark surfaces create more friction
🌲 Yes! Dark surfaces absorb more light energy. That absorbed energy turns into heat, making the surface feel warmer.
Think about what absorption means. When light is absorbed, where does the energy go?
3. A student rubs two sticks together quickly and they start to feel warm. What is causing the heat?
AReflection of light
BAbsorption of sound energy
CFriction between the two surfaces
🌲 Exactly! Rubbing two surfaces together creates friction, and friction produces heat energy.
The sticks are being rubbed together. What happens when two surfaces rub against each other?
