What Is a Fossil? 1 pt
1Which of these is a fossil?
  • AA live snail crawling on a rock
  • BA bone from a living deer found in the woods
  • CThe preserved shape of an ancient fern leaf pressed into a rock millions of years ago
  • DA drawing of a dinosaur on a museum wall
Observation vs. Inference 1 pt
2A scientist looks at a fossil fish and says: "This animal had scales and fins." Then she says: "This fish probably lived in a shallow lake." Which statement is an inference?
  • AThe first statement — scales and fins are visible features she can see
  • BThe second statement — she cannot see the lake; she is using clues to figure out where it lived
  • CBoth are inferences — scientists can never observe fossils directly
  • DNeither — fossils give us no useful information
Rock Layers 1 pt
3A scientist finds fossils of sea animals in a rock layer at the top of a desert cliff. What can she infer about what that place was like long ago?
  • AThe desert has always been a dry desert with no changes
  • BThat area was once covered by water — the fossil sea animals are evidence
  • CThe sea animals walked across the desert and died there
  • DSea animals can live in deserts without water
What Fossils Tell Us 1 pt
4Scientists find fossils of the same type of plant on two different continents that are now separated by an ocean. What might they infer from this?
  • APlants can swim across oceans
  • BThose two continents were once connected, allowing the same plants to grow in both places
  • CThe continents have always been exactly where they are now
  • DThe fossils were placed there by people hundreds of years ago

Fossil Field Report
4 points — Analyze fossil clues and explain your thinking.
🗺️ Fossil Site: Clues Found at Mystery Creek

Clue 1: A fossil of a clam shell, 3 inches wide, found 4 feet underground in a rock layer.
Clue 2: Fossil fish bones found in the same rock layer, near the clam.
Clue 3: Fossil palm tree leaf print found 1 foot underground in a higher rock layer.
Clue 4: Animal tracks pressed into soft mud that hardened into rock, found near the surface.
Note: Deeper rock layers are older. Shallower layers are more recent.

Observations 1 pt
5Pick two clues from the fossil site. Write one observation for each one — describe exactly what you notice without explaining why yet.
💡 Start with: "Clue ___ shows ___. Clue ___ shows ___."
Inferences 1 pt
6Use the clues to make two inferences about what Mystery Creek was like in the past. Explain which clue led you to each inference.
💡 Start with: "I can infer that long ago, this place ___. I know this because Clue ___ shows ___."
Older or Newer? 1 pt
7Were the clam and fish fossils formed before or after the palm leaf fossil? How do you know? Use what you learned about rock layers to explain.
💡 Start with: "The clam and fish fossils are ___ because ___. Rock layers tell us that ___."
Write the Story 1 pt
8Use all four clues to write a short story about how Mystery Creek changed over time. What was it like long ago? What is it like now? Use evidence from the clues to tell the full story.
💡 Start with: "Long ago, Mystery Creek was ___. Over time, ___. Today, the area is ___."
