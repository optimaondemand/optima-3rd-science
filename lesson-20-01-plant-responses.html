📖 The Lesson: How Plants Respond
Read and think carefully — science is all about noticing!
⏱ 12 min
🌱
Section 1
Plants Can't Walk — But They Still Respond

A plant can't get up and walk toward water. It can't move away from the dark. But a plant is not helpless. It responds to the world around it by changing how it grows.

A responseresponseA change caused by something in the environment. happens when something in the environment — called a stimulusstimulusSomething that causes a living thing to respond, like light, water, or gravity. — causes the plant to grow differently. The plant isn't making a choice. Its cells just grow faster on one side, and the plant bends.

🤔
Stop and Think
"Think about the plant in the photo you studied. What do you think made it grow in that curving direction? What was the stimulus, and what was the response?"
🔦
Section 2
Three Stimuli, Three Responses

Plants respond to three main things in their environment.

🔦 Light — Stems and leaves grow toward light. This is called phototropismphototropismWhen a plant bends toward a light source. "Photo" means light, "tropism" means turning.. A plant near a window will slowly lean toward the glass. That reaching helps the leaves collect more sunlight so the plant can make food.

💧 Water — Roots grow toward water in the soil. If one side of the soil is wetter, the roots tend to grow that direction. Scientists call this hydrotropismhydrotropismWhen roots grow toward a water source. "Hydro" means water, "tropism" means turning.. It gets the plant the water it needs to stay alive.

⬇️ Gravity — Roots grow down and stems grow up, no matter which direction you plant a seed. Even if you flip a seedling upside down, its stem will curve back upward. This response to gravitygravityThe force that pulls things toward the ground. Plants use it as a directional guide. is called gravitropismgravitropismWhen roots grow downward and stems grow upward in response to gravity. "Gravi" means gravity, "tropism" means turning..

🌍
Section 3
Responses Help Plants Survive

Every one of these responses has a purpose. Stems reach toward light so leaves can make food. Roots grow toward water so the plant doesn't dry out. Roots grow down so the plant stays anchored in the soil.

None of this is accidental. Plants that respond well to their environmentenvironmentEverything surrounding a living thing — light, water, soil, air, and more. are more likely to survivesurvivalStaying alive. Plant responses help plants get what they need to keep living. and grow.

Real-World Connection: In a dense forest, tall trees block most of the sunlight. Smaller plants close to the ground have adapted to grow very quickly toward any patch of light that breaks through the canopy — phototropism helps them compete for the energy they need.

⭐ Key Concept

Plants respond to light, water, and gravity by changing the direction they grow. These responses help them get what they need to survive — even though they can never move from place to place.

This is structure and function at work: the way a plant's cells grow is the structure, and helping the plant survive is the function.

🤯
Amazing Nature Fact
Some climbing vines can detect which direction a support structure is located and turn their tendrils toward it in under 10 minutes — responding faster to a mechanical touch than most plants respond to light.
Curious? Go Deeper

The Science Behind the Bend. When a plant stem bends toward light, it's not moving like an animal would. What actually happens is that a chemical called auxin travels away from the bright side of the stem. The cells on the shady side get more auxin — and auxin makes cells grow longer. So the shady side grows longer than the bright side, and the stem bends toward the light. It's a slow push, not a pull.

What happens underground? The same kind of chemical signaling happens in roots, but in reverse. Roots grow away from light and toward gravity. If you plant a seed sideways, the root will eventually turn and grow downward — pulled by the same force that keeps your feet on the ground.

🧠 Quick Self-Check
Not graded — just for you! See how much you remember before starting the notebook work.
1. What is phototropism?
AWhen roots grow toward water
BWhen a plant stem bends toward light
CWhen a plant stops growing in winter
🌿 Yes! "Photo" means light. The stem bends toward the light source so leaves can collect more sunlight.
Think about what "photo" means in phototropism. What does a plant need from light — and how does bending toward it help the plant get that?
2. A seedling is planted sideways. After one week, the stem is pointing up and the roots are pointing down. What caused this?
AThe plant responded to gravity
BThe plant responded to water
CThe plant responded to sunlight
🌱 That's right! No matter which way the seed was planted, gravity pulls the roots downward and pushes the stem upward.
Look at the clue: the seed was sideways, but both the stem and roots corrected direction on their own. Which stimulus controls which way is "up" and which is "down"?
3. Why do plant responses — like growing toward light — help a plant survive?
AThey make the plant grow faster than other plants
BThey help the plant move to a better location
CThey help the plant get the resources it needs, like sunlight and water
⭐ Exactly! Each response helps the plant find or reach something it needs — light for food, water for growth, soil for support.
Plants can't move from place to place — but they can grow differently. Which option connects the response to getting a resource the plant actually needs?
