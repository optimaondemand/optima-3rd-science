Case Study: Lake Clearwater
4 points — Read the case study, then answer the questions below.
📄 Case Study

A town built a new factory near Lake Clearwater. Over time, chemicals from the factory flowed into the lake. The water turned brownish-green and smelled bad. Fish populations dropped by half. Water birds that ate the fish had less food and began to leave the area. Scientists tested the water and found high levels of chemical pollution. After studying the data, the town council voted to create a protection zone around the lake, require the factory to use a cleaner process, and hire volunteers to plant native marsh grasses along the shoreline to filter the water naturally.
