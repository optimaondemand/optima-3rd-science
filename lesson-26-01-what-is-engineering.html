📖 The Lesson: What Is Engineering?
Read and think carefully — science is all about noticing!
⏱ 12 min
⚙️
Section 1
Engineers Solve Problems

Look around you. Almost everything you see was designed by an engineerEngineeringUsing science and math to design tools, structures, or systems that solve real problems.. Your pencil. The chair you're sitting in. The zipper on your backpack. Someone figured out a problem — and worked until they found a solution that worked.

EngineeringEngineeringUsing science and math to design tools, structures, or systems that solve real problems. is using what we know about science and math to design things that work better. Engineers don't just guess. They observe, plan, and test — just like scientists do.

🤔
Stop and Think
"What is one object near you right now that someone had to design? What problem does it solve?"
🔄
Section 2
The Design Process

Engineers follow a set of steps called the design processDesign ProcessA step-by-step way engineers plan, build, test, and improve a solution.. First, they identify the problem. Then they plan a solution, build a prototypePrototypeA first working version of a solution, built to test and improve before making a final version., test it, and improve it.

Here's the key part: engineers almost never get it right the first time. Testing shows what doesn't work. Improving is part of the job — not a sign of failure.

💡
Stop and Think
"Why do you think engineers test their prototypes instead of just using the first design they come up with?"
📋
Section 3
Criteria and Constraints

Before building anything, engineers write down two things. First, the criteriaCriteriaThe requirements a solution must meet to be considered successful. — what the solution must do to be successful. Second, the constraintsConstraintA limit on a solution — like cost, time, or the materials you're allowed to use. — the limits they have to work within, like cost or materials.

Imagine designing a lunch bag. A criterion might be "it must keep food cold for 4 hours." A constraint might be "you can only use fabric and one zipper." Knowing both up front helps engineers make good decisions.

Real-World Connection: Every bridge, car, phone, and medical device started with a list of criteria and constraints. The engineer's job is to meet all the criteria while staying within the constraints.

🗺 Tap a step to learn more
Tap any step in the diagram to learn about it.
The engineering design process is a cycle — engineers can go back to any step and improve.
⭐ Key Concept

Engineers follow a design process: define the problem, plan a solution, build a prototype, test it, and improve it. Before they start, they write down the criteria (what success looks like) and constraints (what they're limited to).

Cause and effect is at the heart of engineering — every design choice has a consequence, and testing reveals which ones work.

🤯
Amazing Engineering Fact
The zipper — something on almost every jacket and backpack — took over 40 years and more than 20 failed designs before engineers finally built one that actually worked reliably. The inventor kept testing and improving until he got it right.
Curious? Go Deeper

In 1970, three astronauts on Apollo 13 were in danger. Their spacecraft had lost power, and a carbon dioxide filter had broken. Engineers on the ground had to design a fix — using only the materials already on the spacecraft. They had less than two hours.

They dumped a pile of objects on a table — plastic bags, cardboard, tape, a sock — and said: "Figure out how to fit a square peg in a round hole using only this." Engineers worked until they found a solution. It wasn't pretty, but it worked. The astronauts survived.

That's the design process under pressure. Define the problem. Work with constraints. Test. Improve. Every engineer who has ever fixed something has done a version of the same thing.

1. What is engineering?
ADrawing pictures of buildings for art class
BUsing science and math to design solutions to problems
CFixing only computers and electronic devices
🔧 That's right! Engineering uses science and math to design things that solve real problems — from simple tools to complex systems.
Not quite. Engineering is specifically about using science and math to design solutions — it applies to almost everything we use, not just electronics.
2. An engineer is designing a water bottle. It must hold at least 12 oz. and cost under $3 to make. The cost limit is an example of a —
APrototype
BCriterion
CConstraint
⭐ Yes! A cost limit is a constraint — it's a limit the engineer has to work within. The "hold 12 oz." part would be a criterion.
Think about the two terms. A criterion is what the solution must do to succeed. A constraint is a limit on the solution. Which one is a cost limit?
3. Which step comes FIRST in the engineering design process?
ADefine the problem
BBuild a prototype
CTest your solution
🌿 Exactly! You always start by clearly defining the problem. If you don't know what you're solving, you can't build or test anything yet.
Building and testing are important, but they come later. Engineers always start by defining exactly what the problem is — before anything else.
