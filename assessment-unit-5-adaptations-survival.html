Survival Case Study
4 points — Read and respond.
📄 Case Study: The Brook Trout

Brook trout are fish that live in cold, clear mountain streams. They need water below 65°F to survive. They eat insects and small invertebrates that live in the water. They need clean water with lots of oxygen, shaded pools to rest in, and gravel beds to lay eggs. A logging company wants to cut down the trees along one stream. Without the trees, the stream would warm up, lose its shade, and the soil would wash into the water — clouding it and covering the gravel beds.

Habitat Needs 1 pt
5List three specific things the brook trout needs from its habitat to survive. Use evidence from the case study.
💡 Start with: "The brook trout needs ___, ___, and ___. I know this because the case study says ___."
Limiting Factors 1 pt
6If the trees along the stream are cut down, what limiting factors would affect the brook trout? Explain how each one would hurt its chances of survival.
💡 Start with: "Without the trees, the stream would ___. This would limit the trout because ___."
Food Web Connection 1 pt
7The brook trout eats insects in the stream. If the water becomes cloudy and warm, the insects that the trout eat might also disappear. How would losing its food source affect the trout? What else in the food web might be affected?
💡 Start with: "If the insects disappear, the trout would ___. This would also affect ___ because ___."
Design a Solution 1 pt
8You are an engineer hired to protect the brook trout's habitat. Describe one thing you would do and explain how it would help the trout survive.
💡 Start with: "I would ___ because this would help the brook trout by ___."
